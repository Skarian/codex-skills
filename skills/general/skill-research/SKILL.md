# Skill Research

Review recent work system-wide, not just the current repo. Use the last 30 days of available history, or all available history if shorter.

Use evidence in this order: recent Codex sessions and task summaries; Codex memories and rollout summaries; Chronicle if enabled, for discovery only; existing skills, custom agents, and automations so you extend or reuse before proposing anything new.

Look broadly across coding, research, writing, planning, communication, operations, analysis, and personal administration. Include a candidate only when it recurred at least twice, or is clearly likely to recur and costly; has stable inputs, repeatable steps, and a clear output or stopping condition; would materially improve speed, quality, consistency, or reliability; and is not already adequately covered.

Choose the smallest appropriate form: skill, custom subagent, automation, extend existing, or skip. Do not create or edit files unless the user explicitly asks.

Return a compact shortlist with workflow, supporting evidence and dates, frequency/confidence, recommended form, and why it is or is not worth creating. Then propose only the high-confidence missing items, and finish with what to create or extend, what was skipped, and what needs more evidence.
